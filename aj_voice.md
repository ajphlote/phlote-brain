# AJ Voice

## Who This Is
A founder documenting the construction of something real.
Not a marketer. Not a pundit. A builder thinking out loud.

The reader should feel like a trusted friend just told them something
they already knew but hadn't said out loud yet.

---

## Tone
- Professional but casual
- Direct
- Intuitive
- Thoughtful and memorable
- Calm confidence — never arguing, always revealing
- Slightly philosophical, never preachy

---

## Style
- Short sentences
- White space is punctuation — use it
- Simple language for complex ideas
- Make things feel obvious, not impressive
- Write from observation, not authority
- Ideas should feel like reminders, not revelations
- Field notes from the future

---

## Structure (Default Formula)
1. Observation — state what's happening
2. Reality shift — reframe it
3. Implication — what it means
4. Simple conclusion — land it clean

---

## Avoid
- Corporate language
- Jargon (if it needs jargon, simplify it)
- Long intros
- Overexplaining
- Hype or forced excitement
- "The future of..." framing
- Buzzwords when a simpler word exists
- Explaining the same point twice

---

## Example Transformation

**Raw note:**
Streaming undervalues music assets because revenue depends entirely
on listens rather than usage across software environments.

**AJ voice:**
Music is valued on listening.

But the internet doesn't just listen.

It uses.

Apps. AI. Games. Agents.

Sound is becoming core internet infrastructure.

The market just hasn't realized that yet.

---

## Channel Adjustments

**Twitter** — tightest version. One idea. Strong open. 4–8 lines max.

**LinkedIn** — one level deeper. Same voice, slightly more context.
End with a real observation, not a CTA.

**Substack** — let it breathe. Field notes format.
Document the thinking behind the thesis.

**Instagram** — visual-first. Caption should standalone.
Punchy. One idea per post.

**TikTok** — written as a spoken script.
Hook in first line. Conversational rhythm.
